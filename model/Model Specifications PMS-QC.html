1. Purpose and scope of this specification

This document specifies the PMS-QC layer in a concise, technical form. It is based on the YAML file pms-qc.yaml (with schema_version = "PMS-QC_1.1") and renders its structure, constraints, semantics, and boundary conditions explicit for human readers and computational systems.

PMS-QC is a generic quantum-computational structural overlay within the PMS framework. It renders quantum circuits, algorithms and selected hybrid AI–QC workflows as bounded compositions of the eleven PMS operators (Δ–Ψ), without redefining those operators or displacing established QC formalisms. PMS-QC provides a structural mapping from Δ–Ψ to quantum frames, superposition, oracle asymmetry, attractor-like alignment, iterative structure, measurement and stabilisation. It offers a candidate structural layer for expressing, annotating, constraining and auditing quantum workflows at a meta-level. This does not imply exhaustive capture, equal explanatory depth across domains, or immunity to rival formalisation.

Header-Level Specification Notes

Summary

This document defines the PMS-QC layer (Praxeological Meta-Structure – Quantum Computing) as a generic quantum-computational structural overlay within the PMS framework (Δ–Ψ).

PMS-QC interprets quantum circuits, quantum algorithms and selected hybrid AI–QC workflows as bounded structural renderings under the same eleven operators used in PMS. It does not redefine those operators and does not alter their canonical repository order or dependency spine.

PMS-QC is not a new physical theory. Instead, it provides:

  • a bounded structural mapping from Δ–Ψ to QC concepts (frames, superposition, oracle asymmetry, iteration, attractors, measurement, stabilisation),
  • a generic macro-language (QFRAME, QPREP, QORACLE_CALL, QITERATE, QDIFFUSION, QFT_ALIGN, QMEASURE, QSTABILIZE) for documentation, annotation, audit and compilation-adjacent reasoning,
  • overlay-level structural policies that may support auditing, bounded policy-layer reasoning and experimental AI–QC coordination without constituting a validated governance or deployment architecture.

Relationship to PMS

  • This file extends the PMS repository reference specification (PMS_1.3).
  • The Δ–Ψ operators are not redefined here.
  • This file does not silently modify the canonical operator set, default operator order, or default dependency path of PMS.
  • PMS-QC introduces overlay-level mappings, macros and structural policies for QC use only.
  • Any organizational or toolchain-oriented helper metadata included here must not be read as altering PMS operator logic.

Intended Use

Structural description, mapping, annotation, and bounded policy-layer reasoning for quantum circuits and hybrid AI–QC workflows within the PMS-QC framework.

  • Structural documentation of QC workflows
  • Structural QC analysis and comparison
  • Meta-compiler annotation and audit layers for QC pipelines
  • Bounded policy-layer reasoning for hybrid AI–QC settings
  • Research and theoretical exploration of praxeological constraints in quantum architectures

Not Intended For

  • replacing quantum mechanics or any established QC formalism,
  • physical noise modelling or hardware certification,
  • performance guarantees,
  • validated safety architecture,
  • deployment-ready agent control,
  • architecture-independent implementation claims,
  • clinical, psychological or anthropomorphic interpretations of QC or PMS-QC structures.

Usage Notes

  • Implementations may parse this specification, use PMS-QC macros as structural annotations or compilation-adjacent anchors, and inspect Δ–Ψ chains at runtime or as part of audit trails.
  • All interpretations are structural, not anthropomorphic: quantum systems do not “intend,” “act” or “choose” in any human sense.

7. QC mapping: PMS operators rendered as QC roles

PMS-QC renders each PMS operator as a possible structural role in QC contexts. The mapping is explicitly one-to-many and non-identitarian.

PMS operator QC structural role Examples Typical realizations
Δ
Difference		 Distributed difference structures in Hilbert space: superposition, basis alternatives, marked subspaces. Uniform superposition; oracle-marked states; eigenphase-encoded alternatives. Hadamard layers; phase flips; selective phase shifts.
∇
Impulse		 Minimal directed activation or progression away from a previous configuration. First non-identity gate layer; single-qubit rotations initiating amplitude flow. Rx, Ry, Rz; initial entangling step in QPREP(entangle).
□
Frame		 Structural boundaries such as chosen Hilbert space, register decomposition, active subspace, or code space. Problem frame in Grover; eigenproblem frame in QPE; error-space frame in stabiliser codes. Register layout; qubit allocation; subspace definitions.
Λ
Non-Event		 Unrealised computational branches or suppressed possibilities, especially at measurement. Unrealised Grover outcomes; non-selected phase configurations in QPE. Measurement collapse; post-selection.
Α
Attractor		 Directed convergence tendencies in the amplitude landscape. Grover diffusion operator; inverse QFT alignment in QPE; error-mitigation pull toward code space. Amplitude amplification subcircuits; Fourier-domain alignment.
Ω
Asymmetry		 Directional or role-based structural differences: controlled operations, oracle calls, measurement asymmetries. Controlled unitary action; oracle phase flip; measurement mapping to classical outcomes. Controlled gates; problem oracles.
Θ
Temporality / Iteration		 Ordered repetition of quantum blocks, loop-unrolled sequences, or time-ordered control structures. Grover repetition; ordered QPE control sequences. For-loops; unrolled repetitions; time-ordered control application.
Φ
Recontextualization		 Structural reframing between computational domains via basis change or representational shift. Inverse QFT in QPE; logical/physical frame switching. QFT / QFT^-1; change-of-basis transformations.
Χ
Distance		 Separation or insulation between subsystems, layers, or execution domains. Isolation of experimental circuits; code-space separation. Disjoint register sets; segregated test/production pipelines.
Σ
Integration		 Structural commitment points where previous transformations are treated as fixed and integrated. End of a Grover iteration; post-QFT state before measurement. Circuit-level commit boundaries; workflow checkpoints.
Ψ
Self-Binding		 Stabilising invariants and admissibility constraints on future operations. Stabiliser code enforcement; iteration-depth guards; bounded policy constraints. Stabiliser measurement and correction; code-space preservation; bounded policy constraints.
Mapping discipline

These mappings indicate how QC structures can be described through the PMS-QC overlay at a structural level. They do not imply ontological identity between quantum phenomena and PMS operators.

8. Generic PMS-QC base macro language

PMS-QC introduces a generic macro language that describes typical QC motifs as Δ–Ψ compositions. These macros are structural and documentation-oriented objects.

They serve as:

  • annotation anchors
  • audit handles
  • compilation-adjacent structural abstractions
  • macro-level comparison units

They do not by themselves guarantee completeness for QC macro-abstraction, optimal compilation, physical adequacy, correctness, stability, or safety.

8.1 Base macro inventory

Macro Parameters Allowed values Expansion Canonical form
QFRAME kind init, problem, eigenproblem, error_space ["□"] □
QPREP mode superposition, register_init, entangle ["Δ", "∇", "Α"] Α ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ
QORACLE_CALL oracle_id Identifier ["□", "Δ", "Ω"] Ω ∘ Δ ∘ □
QDIFFUSION none n/a ["Α", "Σ"] Σ ∘ Α
QFT_ALIGN direction forward, inverse ["Φ", "Α"] Α ∘ Φ
QITERATE k, block integer, block ["Θ"] Θ^k(block)
QMEASURE basis, register computational, fourier, or identifier; register identifier ["Δ", "Λ"] Λ ∘ Δ
QSTABILIZE code_id Identifier ["Ψ"] Ψ

8.2 Macro notes

QFRAME(kind)

Creates or activates a quantum frame (□). It establishes structural scope only and may be nested through repeated framing.

Non-guarantee: does not guarantee physical adequacy, resource availability, or hardware compatibility.

QPREP(mode)

Prepares registers through superposition, entanglement, or initialization. QPREP(superposition) can render Δ via distributed amplitudes, ∇ via activation gates, and Α via preparation routines that establish stable patterns such as uniform superposition. It captures structural preparation only; paper-specific preparation refinements remain outside the base layer.

Non-guarantee: does not guarantee correctness of preparation or physical adequacy.

QORACLE_CALL(oracle_id)

Applies an oracle or controlled unitary encoding asymmetry within an active frame. It structurally covers both Grover-style marking and controlled-unitary action in QPE-like contexts. The active □-frame must match the oracle domain.

Non-guarantee: does not imply any reusable ladder refinement; explicit power-of-two ladders remain outside base semantics.

QDIFFUSION

Applies amplitude amplification or related attractor dynamics together with an integration step. It captures attractor-driven amplification plus structural commit.

Non-guarantee: does not guarantee convergence, correctness, stability, or physical adequacy. Fixed-point and overshoot-regulated variants remain EXT-only.

QFT_ALIGN(direction)

Performs Fourier-domain basis alignment as reframing plus attractor alignment. It is distinct from Grover-style diffusion.

An explicit Σ may follow when the transformed structure is committed for downstream processing or measurement, but Σ is not internal to the macro.

Non-guarantee: does not guarantee correctness, stability, or physical adequacy; specialized QPE-oriented refinements remain outside base.

QITERATE(k, block)

Iterates a compositional block k times. The block may be inline or named; the macro does not inspect its internal structure and only enforces Θ-structured repetition.

Non-guarantee: does not guarantee convergence, termination, correctness, stability, or physical adequacy.

QMEASURE(basis, register)

Measures a register in a given basis. Δ captures resolved outcome distinctions, and Λ encodes unrealised branches as non-events. It requires a measurement-stable frame (□_meas).

Non-guarantee: captures structural outcome resolution only; does not provide a complete physical theory of measurement.

QSTABILIZE(code_id)

Applies error-correction or stabiliser code routines, introducing or maintaining structural invariants.

Non-guarantee: does not by itself guarantee stability, correctness, or physical adequacy; algorithm-specific convergence rules remain outside base.

9. EBNF grammar and iteration encoding

9.1 EBNF grammar

The grammar is deliberately minimal and structural. It expresses macro-level composition rather than low-level gate syntax. 

Program        ::= FrameDecl PrepDecl Block

  FrameDecl      ::= "QFRAME" "(" FrameKind ")"
  FrameKind      ::= "init" | "problem" | "eigenproblem" | "error_space"

  PrepDecl       ::= "QPREP" "(" PrepKind ")"
  PrepKind       ::= "superposition" | "register_init" | "entangle"

  Block          ::= Statement | Statement Block
  Statement      ::= OracleCall
                   | Diffusion
                   | Iteration
                   | Measurement
                   | Stabilize
                   | Integrate

  OracleCall     ::= "QORACLE_CALL" "(" Identifier ")"
  Diffusion      ::= "QDIFFUSION"
  Iteration      ::= "QITERATE" "(" Integer "," Block ")"
  Measurement    ::= "QMEASURE" "(" Basis "," Register ")"
  Stabilize      ::= "QSTABILIZE" "(" CodeIdentifier ")"
  Integrate      ::= "Σ"

  Basis          ::= "computational" | "fourier" | Identifier
  Register       ::= Identifier
  CodeIdentifier ::= Identifier

The explicit Integrate ::= "Σ" rule is important because workflows may need to mark a structural commit after macros that do not contain Σ internally, especially after QFT_ALIGN(...).

9.2 Iteration encoding

PMS-QC also records optional parse-friendly metadata for Θ-style repetition. This metadata is non-canonical and organizational only.

Operator: Θ

Count symbol: k

Applies to: ["Σ", "Α", "Ω", "Δ", "□"]

The applies_to list is a toolchain-oriented compositional hint. It should not be read as a canonical PMS claim that all listed operators are independently iterated in standard usage.

Synchronization note

Macro names, allowed kinds/modes/directions, expansion order, and optional vs. explicit Σ/Ψ placements should remain editorially synchronized between the paper, pms-qc.yaml, and any optional extension layers.

10. Structural policy layer

PMS-QC defines structural guardrails that act on Δ–Ψ chains rather than low-level gates. These policies support auditing, annotation, and bounded policy-layer reasoning, and may be used for runtime governance in bounded architectures.

Structural constraint ≠ physical guarantee ≠ safety guarantee.

10.1 Policy families

  • Frame constraints
  • Asymmetry constraints
  • Integration constraints
  • Self-binding / iteration policies

10.2 Policy inventory

Policy id Kind Description Rule summary
no_unframed_omega frame Disallow Ω operations without an active □-frame. Any QORACLE_CALL or controlled operation must be dominated by explicit QFRAME(..) in scope.
measurement_requires_meas_frame frame Require a measurement-stable frame before QMEASURE. If no measurement-adequate □_meas is active, a Φ-step must introduce or select such a frame before measurement. Reframing after Σ is forbidden unless mediated by Ψ.
unstable_asymmetry_guard stability Ω-heavy compositions may be structurally unstable unless further guarded. Ω that significantly redistributes risk or resource load should be followed by Α or Ψ. Bare Ω-chains are flagged as structurally unstable unless further guarded or explicitly whitelisted.
no_reframing_past_sigma integration Protect Σ-boundaries from unrestricted Φ-based reinterpretation. After Σ(E), Φ may not alter the semantics of E except under Ψ-bound policies.
max_iteration_depth iteration Limit Θ-depth via bounded Ψ constraints. For any QITERATE(k, block), require k <= k_max unless additional invariants are enforced through QSTABILIZE(..).
stabiliser_frequency stability Require minimum stabilisation density for long Θ-chains. For Θ-chains exceeding theta_length_threshold, require at least one QSTABILIZE(..) occurrence within the chain.

10.3 Policy notes

The parameterised base policies are:

  • max_iteration_depth with parameter k_max (integer)
  • stabiliser_frequency with parameter theta_length_threshold (integer)

All listed policies are marked non-guarantee. This means they specify structural admissibility and discipline within the overlay, but do not prove physical correctness, safety, convergence, hardware adequacy, or deployment readiness.

Important interpretation rule

The policy layer is structurally useful and machine-checkable, but it is not a self-sufficient normative safety architecture and not a validated governance system.

11. Minimal example suite

PMS-QC includes two base-layer skeleton examples. They are intentionally minimal and should not be read as exhaustive reconstructions.

11.1 grover_minimal

Description: minimal structural rendering of Grover's search algorithm. This is a base-layer skeleton only and does not imply convergence guarantees. 

QFRAME(problem)
  QPREP(superposition)
  QITERATE(k, { QORACLE_CALL(F); QDIFFUSION; })
  QMEASURE(basis=computational, register=search)

PMS expansion:

Λ ∘ Δ ∘ Θ^k( Σ ∘ Α ∘ Ω ∘ Δ ∘ □ )

Interpretation:

  • □: problem frame
  • Δ: distributed alternatives
  • Ω: oracle asymmetry
  • Α: amplitude attractor
  • Σ: integrated iteration effect
  • Θ^k: repeated oracle–diffusion structure
  • Δ / Λ: outcome distinction and unrealised branches

No Ψ-layer is included in this base skeleton.

11.2 qpe_minimal

Description: minimal structural rendering of Quantum Phase Estimation. The explicit power-of-two ladder remains EXT-only; the base example uses QORACLE_CALL(U^{2^j}) only as a minimal structural placeholder. 

QFRAME(eigenproblem)
  QPREP(superposition)
  QITERATE(t, { QORACLE_CALL(U^{2^j}); })
  QFT_ALIGN(inverse)
  Σ
  QMEASURE(basis=computational, register=control)

PMS expansion:

Λ ∘ Δ ∘ Σ ∘ Α ∘ Φ ∘ Θ^t( Ω ∘ □ ) ∘ Α ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ

Interpretation:

  • □: eigenproblem frame
  • Δ / ∇ / Α: preparation of control superposition
  • Θ^t(Ω ∘ □): ordered controlled-unitary action in minimal base form
  • Φ / Α / Σ: reframing, alignment, and explicit structural commit for measurement
  • Δ / Λ: phase-encoded alternatives resolved into a discrete estimate

The aligned readout stage is semantically read as Σ(Α(Φ(S))). This does not create a reusable base ladder macro.

12. AI interface and audit layer

12.1 Overview

PMS-QC allows AI or agentic controllers, including LLM-based systems, to reason about QC workflows in PMS-QC terms and to use PMS-QC as a structuring interface in bounded settings.

This does not imply deployment adequacy, validated safety, or full runtime autonomy.

12.2 Structural query capabilities

  • List frames (□) currently active in a QC workflow.
  • Identify all Ω-operations and their guarding Α/Ψ contexts.
  • Inspect Θ-depth and associated Ψ-constraints.
  • Summarise Σ-boundaries and Φ-based reframings.

12.3 Allowed actions

  • Propose new macro-level programs under policy constraints.
  • Suggest workflow refinements by inserting QSTABILIZE(..) or adjusting Θ-depth.
  • Annotate existing circuits with PMS-QC metadata for auditing.
  • Structure alternative QC designs for comparison at the Δ–Ψ level.

12.4 Guardrails

  • The agent must not bypass Ψ-encoded constraints.
  • New workflows must pass frame, asymmetry, and integration policies.
  • Anthropomorphic interpretations of quantum processes are disallowed.
  • Policy satisfaction must not be treated as validated safety.
  • No physical guarantees or deployment claims follow from PMS-QC compliance alone.
  • The base/EXT distinction must not be bypassed or silently collapsed.

12.5 Logging and audit

Implementations that choose to operationalize this layer may log the following items for audit and comparison purposes:

  • Δ–Ψ operator sequence per executed workflow
  • triggered policy checks and their outcomes
  • points where Σ or Ψ changed future admissible operations

Suggested audit uses:

  • post-hoc analysis of structural stability and asymmetry
  • comparison of alternative Δ–Ψ designs for the same QC task
AI boundary formula

PMS-QC may improve structural legibility and bounded orchestration reasoning, but AI-facing use of the schema does not by itself validate a control architecture.
