# PMS-QC

## Praxeological Meta-Structure for Quantum Computing

**PMS-QC** is a technical bridge overlay of the **Praxeological Meta-Structure Theory (PMS)**. It applies the canonical PMS operator grammar (Δ–Ψ) as a formal structural and contextual layer for quantum-computational workflows.

PMS-QC does **not** modify, extend, reinterpret, or replace the PMS operator set. It is an operator-strict bridge layer that uses PMS as its structural foundation.

Its central thesis is:

> Quantum computation can be structurally annotated through PMS operators without reducing PMS to physics, and without replacing quantum-mechanical, circuit-theoretic, or mathematical formalisms.

PMS-QC investigates structural correspondences between PMS operators — especially frame semantics (□), distinction (Δ), asymmetry (Ω), temporality (Θ), recontextualization (Φ), isolation (Χ), integration (Σ), and stabilization / self-binding (Ψ) — and quantum-computational structures such as:

- quantum circuits,
- basis and context switching,
- superposition and entanglement boundaries,
- oracle asymmetry,
- quantum measurement,
- hybrid classical–quantum workflows,
- stabilizer-oriented routines,
- and algorithmic iteration.

PMS-QC therefore treats quantum computation as a domain in which operational coherence, contextual framing, boundary conditions, and structural constraints can be explicitly modeled.

It remains a **bridge claim**, not a physical proof, compiler proof, correctness proof, noise model, or deployment framework.

## 27. Final Note

PMS-QC does not replace quantum computing theory.

It makes quantum-computational workflows structurally readable as framed, constrained, asymmetric, iterative, recontextualizing, measurable, and stabilizable processes.

A PMS-QC rendering becomes useful when it clarifies:

- where a frame begins,
- where preparation occurs,
- where asymmetry enters,
- where repetition accumulates,
- where basis or context shifts,
- where measurement collapses alternatives,
- where integration is staged,
- and where stabilization or invariant structure is required.

PMS-QC becomes structurally invalid when it:

- treats structural mapping as physical proof,
- treats macro rendering as algorithm validation,
- treats examples as convergence guarantees,
- treats PMS-QC-EXT as base semantics,
- treats QC structures anthropomorphically,
- replaces domain formalisms,
- or uses bridge reach as PMS Base validation.

The compact boundary is:

> PMS-QC is not quantum mechanics.  
> It is a structural bridge layer for making quantum-computational workflows PMS-legible without claiming to replace or validate their physical formalism.
